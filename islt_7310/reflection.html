Personal Reflection



A little bit about my education journey:


Currently in my career I'm working at a company called ThermAvant. I do IT support and a variety of other things. It has been a huge learning experience. I love that I'm able to get real world experience in my degree field while still in school!


My experience so far with the Mizzou IT and CS departments has been great. I love having a mix of IT and CS classes. I tend to notice that IT can be a bit more of practical application while computer science is more theorey. I really enjoy both aspects and think both are extermely valuable skills to have.


There are 2 areas in which I have seen the most dramatic imporvements over the course of my college journey. The first one is C programming and algorithm design. Learning the C programming language from the ground up is a challenging yet rewarding task. It has taught me so much about programming and is my favorite language so far. Then being able to apply that knowledge to designing and implementing algorithms is very interesting to me.


The second area that I have seen the most imporvement in is web development. Before learning web development, I didn't think much of it. Now I look at it as so much more than that. It can be extermely complex when you begin to learn more about implementing databases, api's, different javascript frameworks, user authentication, and so much more. For me, web development will be a field in which I'm always growing and wanting to learn more.
